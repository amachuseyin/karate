What is Karate?

Introduction to Karate

Karate is a martial art developed in the Ryukyu Kingdom. It was developed from the indigenous Ryukyuan martial arts under the influence of Kung Fu, particularly Fujian White Crane. Karate is now predominantly a striking art using punching, kicking, knee strikes, elbow strikes, and open-hand techniques such as knife-hands, spear-hands, and palm-heel strikes. Historically, and in some modern styles, grappling, throws, joint locks, restraints, and vital-point strikes are also taught.

Philosophy and Principles

The philosophical values of karate are based on Bushido. The Karate philosophy tends to emphasize self-control, discipline, and defensive rather than offensive maneuvers. The primary goal of karate is self-improvement over physical confrontation.

Benefits of Practicing Karate

Practicing karate can improve physical fitness, mental discipline, and emotional stability. It also provides practical self-defense techniques. Participants of all ages learn to respect themselves and others, enhancing their overall wellbeing and approach to life's challenges.
